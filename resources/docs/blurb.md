# Datalevin: The Definitive Guide to Logical and Intelligent Databases

Datalevin is a compact open-source database system, six years in the making,
built for the age of AI, where agentic applications need durable facts, logical
queries, graph traversal, key-value speed, full-text search, vector retrieval,
and operational simplicity in one place.

This book teaches Datalevin from first principles. It starts with entities,
attributes, datoms, transactions, Datalog, and LMDB-backed storage, then moves
through schema design, modeling patterns, indexes, search, vector similarity,
performance, durability, and production operations.

The final part of the book shows how to leverage the database principles to
design and build a state of the art persistent agentic memory system, detailing
episodes, semantic facts, fact consolidation, working memory, recall pipelines,
truth maintenance, and stateful AI applications.

## About the Author

Dr. Huahai Yang is the creator and maintainer of Datalevin. His work on
Datalevin grew from practical needs at Juji, a conversational agent company he
co-founded, where intelligent applications required fast retrieval, persistent
memory, and flexible representations of user and agent state. He writes and
builds at the intersection of databases, intelligent systems, and human-computer
interaction. He holds a Ph.D. from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor; was a
faculty member at University at Albany, SUNY; and a research staff member at IBM
Almaden Research Center. You can follow him @huahaiy.
